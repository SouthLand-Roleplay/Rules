In-Game Rules:

  1. Common Courtesy
    • All players should treat others with respect. You’re not allowed to break the rules because someone else did. You shouldn’t disrupt, stall or void roleplay unless an administrator tells you too. Examples;
      • Quitting the game to avoid roleplay
      • Repeatedly refusing to roleplay / Avoiding roleplay
      • Bugs, Dsync issues are to be roleplayed out.
      • Assets are to not be transferred without approval by Administration.

    If you come across a bug or exploit of any kind in game you’re required to report it. Exploiting it and using it will result in a permanent ban and it’s your obligation to report rule-breakers as well.

  2. Character Customistation
    • Players must create a realistic character using the customization system. Admins may request a player to change their character's customization if they deem it unfit for the server environment.
    • Characters must dress appropriately for the weather, or at least roleplay the downsides of wearing inappropriate clothes. The current weather can be seen by using the /weather command.
    • Players must only obstruct their face when they have an in-character reason to do so and it makes sense to have their face obstructed where they are.

    Examples of this rule being broken:

    • Dressing your character to resemble an alien.
    • Making your body invisible or intentionally glitched.
    • Wearing a balaclava while hanging out on the beach.
    • Wearing a winter jacket during the summer.
  3. Metagaming
    • Metagaming is defined as using out of character information in character. Inciting someone else to metagame is against this rule. Examples:

    Shneako reads something from out of character chat and begins to roleplay knowing that in-character.
    Sheanko reads something from twitch chat and begins to roleplay knowing that in-character.

    Using out of game communications like Discord, Skype, etc. to communicate in character while in-game is forbidden.

    All characters you create cannot interact with each other in any way. You may not use a third party to transfer IC information from one of your characters to another one of your characters either. You may not reference any of your other characters.

    Insulting another player through Out of Character chat or any form of chat is not allowed (e.g., Twitch, Discord, etc.

    Failing to roleplay a car crash, unless it was caused by desync (contact an admin through /report to void a car crash if both parties agree.

    Leaving the server during an active scene is not allowed, you must wait until the scene has concluded and that an ample amount of time has passed.

  4. Powergaming
    • Powergaming can be defined as performing acts that are not human like; forcing roleplay on others without giving them a fair chance; making up things which did not happen so that you can benefit, roleplaying things which are not possible script-wise so that you can gain an unfair advantage. Examples;
      • Talking/Organizing/Calling out locations while downed.
      • Finding the quickest way to make money, rather than roleplaying around the mechanic.
      • The use of /me and /do should be used as much as possible to help aid your scenarios.
      • Driving at unrealistic speeds with no character reason over and over.
  5. Deathmatching
    • Killing another person without a sufficient roleplay reason (deathmatching) is not allowed. Unnecessary provoking falls under this rule as well. Terrorism is strictly forbidden.
    • This also includes revenge killing — when you respawn after being executed and then kill (or at least attempt to) the person who has just murdered you.
    • Your In Character memory is wiped off everything that led up to your death when you are killed, so you should forget about your attacker. Examples;
      • Attacking another player/group without any story/roleplay leading up to it.
      • Attacking another player/group using a Vehicle with the intent to kill/knock them out without valid reasoning or initiation.
      • Sheanko has killed you over a legitimate in character reason and you respawned without being saved by the Police Department/Fire Department so you get a gun from your house and go back and kill him.
      • You provoke a fist fight with two people who are unarmed and then you pull your firearm out and kill them.
      • Players cannot provoke a situation or another player without valid reasons behind it.
  6. Robbing and Scamming
    • Robbing is defined as taking someone’s possessions (money/items, etc) by force or threat of force. Scamming is defined as offering a good or service and then refusing to pay or give it. Robberies and scams must be strictly done in character.
      Players are unable to force other players to withdraw money from an automated teller, You must always have a valid reasoning behind robbing someone.
      Players are unable to force another player to sell them their vehicle via script commands during a robbery.
      • Players are unable to force other players to hand over assets that aren’t present in the area.
      • All robberies must be roleplayed using the proper /me's and /do's.
      • Players may not scam vehicles or properties.
      • Players may scam for a maximum amount of $20,000 and nothing greater.
      • Players may not rob items from the Police Department and Fire Department.
      • Players can't commit a robbery while operating a luxury, high-end, or sports car.
      • Players are able to rob all of the cash you are carrying on your persona. Avoid walking around with twenty thousand dollars unless you don’t care about losing it.
      • Looting people who are dead is forbidden.
      • A group who splits up shouldn't be hitting a house in 4 different areas of the map to try and spread police out just so they can succeed.
      • You may not rob a player while they are performing a scripted job on the server, ex: garbage pickup.
      • Only tell a player to 'put their hands up' or 'don't move' solely if you intend to rob them, not if you intend to kill them.
      • Chain robberies on your character unless perfectly roleplayed will be considered unrealistic and poor portrayal of a character. You may only do up to one robbery per 4 hours. Group robberies count together the rule doesn't apply to just an individual, it applies to an entire group you're with. Rather you're the getaway driver, burglar, etc. You may not kill someone during a robbery unless they bring it upon themselves. Players must show value for their life and your character's life is important.
  7. New Life Rule (NLR)
    • If you’re downed and respawn at the hospital your character forgets the immediate events leading up to being down in the current scenario. A player may not return to the area of their death for at least 15 minutes or attempt to retrieve lost possessions from involved players. If you happen to Character Kill, you may not make a new character and revenge kill for your deceased character.
    • If you are downed and respawn at the hospital your character forgets the immediate events leading up to being downed in the current scenario. You may not respawn if you’ve been advised that police or EMS is on the way to your scene.

      • You must try and contact medical help of some sort while incapacitated.

  8. Abuse of Game Physics
    • Grand Theft Auto Five physics and movements in-game are hardcoded and cannot be changed via scripting so the following is forbidden to avoid non-roleplay situations:
      Bunnyhopping (I.e: jumping while running around to gain speed in a situation)
      Spam Punching (I.e: spam clicking mouse 1 in a fight and not giving someone a chance to react.)
      • Combat Rolling (I.e: rolling in a shootout, etc.)
      • Olympic Swimming (I.e: swimming without roleplay any exhaustion)
      • Abuse of jobs (I.e: spawning a job vehicle to evade police, etc).
  9. Third Party Modifications
    • Third party modifications are defined as using a program, software that gives you an unfair advantage over other players. This includes the following: mods, trainers, crosshair monitor, tracers, etc.
    • ALSO AI files in your mods folder! There will be a script inside the game that catches an AI file and a mod file that contains unlimited stamina.
  10. Safezones
    • A no crime zone is an area where player(s) may not commit any crimes. If the player that you’re attacking flees to a safezone then the roleplay is allowed to continue.
      • City Hall
      • All government offices, law enforcement offices, hospitals, and banks. (Including the surrounding streets and sidewalks)
      • All Clothing Stores. (Inside Only)
      • Department of Motor Vehicles
      • Mechanic Shops
      • Car Dealerships. (Inside Only)
      • Spawn Locations.
      • Player Owned Businesses. ( The sidewalks and parking lots connected to the businesses also intended to be greenzones, it ends at the street. )
      • All parties excluding block parties & house parties.
      • Parking garages/impounds (where you store your vehicle).
  11. Not Valuing Life
    • All players must value their characters life:
      • Treat your character as you would treat yourself in real life. Be mindful of your character's life at all times.
      • If you are being held at gunpoint you are to do whatever they instruct you to.
      • If someone has a hostage during a situation, you must value the hostage's life and you would want the safety of the hostage.
    • Examples of when you’re not in direct danger:
      • When you’re in a vehicle and the engine isn’t stalled.
      • When you have a firearm drawn and facing the other person before they attack.
      • When the attacker’s view is obstructed by an object or when they turn their back on you.
  12. Criminal Activities
    • Criminal activities are limited to 4 players max at a time.
    • Relaying information to or between characters (lookouts, radio communications) counts you as part of that scenario.
    • Choosing shooters/grinders over roleplayers is not acceptable.
    • Whenever characters are brought into police custody and a second group of four is formed to attempt a prison break or transport break, that second group is not allowed to intervene until the prison transport begins. Doing so with small weapons or an unthought out plan is also NVL.
  13. Multiple Characters(from the same player) in one gang/group)
    • This is not allowed. In general, try to avoid creating multiple characters involved in the same story/scenarios and could use the same information to push their goals.
    • If you're character end ups CK'ing in a faction and you still want to be apart of the same faction, you must start off your new character as a **RESIDENT AND/OR MEMBER** and work your way back up to Leader. This causes more roleplay and character development/storylines when it comes down to faction RP. Also creates more backdooring opportunities for the people who want to get that leadership role in the faction. Any failure to do so will result in a punishment, which could result in a faction strike, or a ban. Also, when you now make a new character it CANNOT be related to the previous character you had before. **THIS ONLY APPLIES IN CHARACTER**
  14. Corruption. (IC)
    • Giving guns out from being in the Police Department or Fire Department to others in the server is classed as Corruption and will be banned for it. No Warnings. Taking a large amount of money, weapons, items etc. from someone and not reporting it to staff.

/me's and /do's

The Correct Way To Use /me, /ame, /my & /do Within Roleplay​.

I'm going to make a short simple guide on how to correctly use /me's & /do's within roleplay. I'll be listing examples so you can get a better understanding on how to separate the two from each other. Refrain from swearing in your /me, /ame, /my & /do as that is the incorrect way to engage into roleplay.

/me - are usually used to describe your side of roleplay, your character, your actions, etc. When using a /me, please ensure that the first word is ALWAYS lowercase due to when you hit ENTER on a /me, your character name already starts the /me sentence off with your Firstname Lastname in capital letters. Also, please ensure there is a period at the end of your /me to complete the sentence. There shouldn't be any scenarios where a question mark (?) or exclamation point (!) is needed in a /me, they should always end in a period. You could also use /ame instead of /me which will place the /me above your character instead of in the chat. Beware, only people close to you could see this action. If they are too far, the text above your character will be too small to read. Try to be descriptive as possible in your /me. You could also use a /my, which is a /me that claims ownership over something in your actions.

Correct Way To Use A /me, /ame, /my (simple):

  • /me places his hands into his pockets.
  • /me shakes the male's hand.
  • /me scans the environment out of paranoia.
  • /me bends down and ties her shoes.
  • /me smells the marijuana in the air.
  • /me yanks up his Dickies branded jeans.
  • /me spots Delonte across the street.
  • /ame keeps her eyes focused on the road while she drives.
  • /ame keeps her hand in the Doritos branded bag of chips.
  • /my bucket had would be red.
  • /my pants would be sagging.
  • /my feet would stink when he removes his shoes.

When using a /do, you can use question marks (?) to question roleplay and periods (.) to describe roleplay on your end. If you're not certain what's going on within roleplay, you can use a /do to figure out what your character see, hear, smell, etc. by asking a question like the examples below. You use periods in /do when you are describing your side of roleplay, your character, your personal belongings, etc.
